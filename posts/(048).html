I was looking at a pomegranate and all I could think about was how grateful I was for the pomegranate.

It spent it's life following a harrowing journey of push and pull, pushed by pain and pulled by desire, to carefully craft its seeds that are so carelessly sowed. It thought it was perfect, it thought it was god–for all it did was what it knew what to do, and to continue you must necessarily believe you do all you may. What do you do when you stop believing?


It is not the question of whether or not to live that you must answer a priori to all else–but rather, the question of whether you believe you live in logical consistency–for if you do not believe that your world is consistent, then you cannot believe you can make a decision. 

You must first decide you do not live in a paradox.
